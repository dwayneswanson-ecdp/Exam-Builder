I needed a better exam solution

Paper exams had stopped working. Students passed around “scratch” notes, hid phones under the table, and handed in copy–paste identical answers. The exam wasn’t measuring knowledge anymore – it was just a game of who could outsmart the rules.

Instead of fighting that with more paper, more surveillance and more frustration, I decided to build an online exam space that makes cheating harder and thinking the focus. The goal is not to catch students cheating, but to design an environment where it’s simply more efficient to do the work than to look for loopholes.

The classroom problem

  • Students coordinated answers via notes and phones despite clear instructions.
  • Paper exams generated a lot of waste.
  • Marking handwritten copies took too long for a quick feedback loop.

The solution

  • Students focus on content, not on finding ways around the rules.
  • Light login, a straightforward exam page, and a report emailed to the teacher.
  • QCM questions are pre-graded so teachers can focus on written / subjective parts.

What’s included in this beta

Snapshot of the current beta

Everything you need to run a strict, focused exam in an in-person classroom.

  • Guided exam creation flow with basic templates.
  • QCU, QCM and open-ended questions.
  • Time-limited exams with a clear countdown.
  • Full-screen mode with penalties if the window loses focus.
  • Protection against overlays and extra windows on top of the exam.
  • No copy–paste in answer fields.
  • Login page with passcode and confirmation page.
  • Email-based exam delivery straight to the instructor’s inbox.

For instructors

What you receive after each exam

At the end of the exam, a detailed report is emailed to the instructor with the student’s answers, automatic scoring for Part A, and a structured space for feedback.

Automatic exam report by email

When a student submits, the platform generates an HTML report that is sent directly to the instructor’s inbox. It includes:

  • Student identity (name, email, class code)
  • Score breakdown for the vocabulary section (Part A)
  • The full written answer (Part B) in a readable format
  • A simple grid for manual scoring and comments

In this beta version, no exam data is stored on a central server. Each report is delivered by email so instructors can archive, annotate, or export it using their usual tools.
