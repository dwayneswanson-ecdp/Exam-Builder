Online Exam Platform
About Instructor Area
For schools & program directors

Secure, flexible online exams for your students

This platform was created to standardise exam practices, simplify the work of teachers, and give the school a clear, auditable framework for online assessments.

Why this platform?

  • Harmonise exam formats across classes and teachers.
  • Improve exam security and traceability (time, settings, versions).
  • Offer multiple question types, including QCU/QCM and open-ended answers.
  • Make exam creation simple for instructors, with no technical skills required.

Benefits for the school

  • Centralised exam creation and storage.
  • Consistent grading and easier moderation between instructors.
  • Clear audit trail (dates, versions, settings).
  • Possibility to reuse and adapt past exams from a question bank.

Benefits for teachers & students

  • Simple, guided interface to build exams step by step.
  • Different question formats, including multimedia content.
  • Fine control over time limits, difficulty and scoring.
  • Comfortable online exam experience for students.

Instructor Area – How exams are created

This section explains the instructor workflow. It is also where we can later add forms so teachers can submit exam information directly.

Step 1

Set up the exam

Teachers start by defining the global parameters of the exam:

  • School year, class and subject.
  • Exam category (mid-term, final, continuous assessment, etc.).
  • Type of application (desktop or web) and platform.
  • Exam date, start time, duration and time zone.
  • Advanced options such as custom time limits, incompressible duration, anonymous correction or copy/paste policy.
Step 2

Choose question types – QCU & QCM

The platform supports both single-choice and multiple-choice questions:

  • Create copy assessments or build MCQs question by question.
  • Integrate multimedia content (images, videos, graphs).
  • Adjust difficulty levels and time allocated per question.
  • Decide whether to show or hide the scoring scale and barème.
Step 2 (continued)

Open-ended answer evaluations

In addition to MCQs, teachers can use open-response questions that let students express themselves freely in writing. This is ideal for:

  • Dissertations and essays.
  • Case studies and business scenarios.
  • Situational judgement questions.
Step 3

Build the exam content

Once the structure and question types are chosen, instructors build the exam itself:

  • Write general instructions for each part of the exam.
  • Add questions one by one or import them from existing documents.
  • Mix QCU, QCM and open-ended questions in a single exam.
  • Optionally tag questions by topic or learning outcome.
Step 4

Publish, run and correct

Before publishing, teachers can preview the exam and check all settings. Once the exam is live:

  • Students access the exam at the defined time from the chosen platform.
  • Answers are stored securely and can be corrected online.
  • Anonymous marking is possible when required.
  • Grades and feedback can be exported for the school’s systems.
