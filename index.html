Why this exam space exists

Paper exams had stopped working. Students passed around “scratch” notes, hid phones under the table, and handed in copy–paste identical answers. The exam wasn’t really measuring knowledge anymore – it was just a game of who could outsmart the rules.

Instead of trying to fix that with more paper, more surveillance and more frustration, I started building an online exam space that makes cheating harder and thinking the focus. The goal is not to catch students cheating, but to design an environment where it’s simply more efficient to do the work than to look for loopholes.

Just as important, this space is meant to re-value the students who show up and do the work day after day. Those who prepare, participate and arrive on time deserve grades that reflect that effort. Students who do not engage in the same way should not automatically receive the same marks. The aim is to reward consistent, honest work rather than demoralise the students who actually try.

The classroom problem

  • Students coordinated answers via notes and phones despite clear instructions.
  • Paper exams generated a lot of waste and extra logistics.
  • Marking handwritten copies took too long for a quick feedback loop.

What this beta aims to do

  • Create a focused, full-screen exam space for in-person classes.
  • Make cheating less convenient than simply answering the questions.
  • Give instructors a clear, structured report they can mark quickly.
  • Re-valorise students who consistently do the work, instead of demoralising them with unfair grade inflation.

For instructors

Instructor tools at a glance

Behind the scenes, the beta focuses on two things: a simple way to configure a strict exam, and a clean report sent directly to your inbox.

Backend & exam controls

Everything you need to run a strict, focused exam in an in-person classroom.

  • Guided exam creation flow with basic templates.
  • QCU, QCM and open-ended questions in the same exam.
  • Time-limited exams with a visible countdown.
  • Full-screen mode with penalties if the window loses focus.
  • Protection against overlays and extra windows on top of the exam.
  • No copy–paste in answer fields.
  • Login page with passcode and identity confirmation.
  • Email-based exam delivery straight to the instructor’s inbox.

Automatic exam report by email

When a student submits, the platform generates an HTML report that is sent directly to the instructor’s inbox. It includes:

  • Student identity (name, email, class code)
  • Score breakdown for the vocabulary section (Part A)
  • The full written answer (Part B) in a readable format
  • A simple grid for manual scoring and comments

In this beta version, no exam data is stored on a central server. Each report is delivered by email so instructors can archive, annotate, or export it using their usual tools.
